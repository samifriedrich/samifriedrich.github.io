About Me

Hi, I'm Sami. I thrive at the intersection of science and technology, particularly when it comes to building tools and resources that help researchers extract, explore, and visualize complex datasets.

I have over 10 years of research experience spanning neuroscience, molecular biology, genomics, and transcriptomics.

  • BS in Neurobiology from the University of Texas at Austin
  • PhD in Behavioral Neuroscience from Oregon Health and Science University

My earliest heroes were Ms. Frizzle and Bill Nye. Their enthusiasm for the scientific method and teaching are inextricably woven into the work I do. I believe science should be accessible, so I approach every collaboration, teaching, mentoring, and outreach opportunity as a chance to practice the art of simplifying complex ideas into engaging insights and shared understanding.

Interested in learning more? Check out some of my guided workshops, recorded seminars, outreach materials, and coding projects below.
